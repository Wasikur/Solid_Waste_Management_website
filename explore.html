Solid Waste Management Plant, Dibrugarh

Assam's first solid waste management project was inaugurated by Union Minister Rameswar Teli in the Thakurthan area at Lekai. This area is located on the outskirts of Dibrugarh. For a long time, the residents had been facing a lot of problems due to the unavailability of proper garbage disposal unit."

>

Earlier, garbage and waste materials were dumped in the Maijan area near river Brahmaputra in Dibrugarh, causing pollution to the entire area. Hence, due to this, the Dibrugarh district have come up with the project and it was successfully inaugurated today. The budget for the building of the project is said to be Rs. 75.03 crores on a 28.3-hectare plot of land. The project was built under the Assam Urban Infrastructure Investment Programme (AUIIP). The solid waste management project is capable of converting waste of 100 metric tonnes per day (TPD) into compost. The project began in 2017 under AUIIP at Lekai.

>

The town of Dibrugarh had been dumping waste at the Maijan Ghat along the Brahmaputra river until now in an unscientific matter. The 22 municipalities of Dibrugarh town produce about 80 metric tonnes of solid waste every day.


Purpose of choosing the process of bio-methanation and pyrolysis for managing the waste generated.

1. This is a very simple and inexpensive technology.
2. It can be used for continuous production of biofuels and organic fertilizer.
3. The operating conditions are moderate and less severe.
4. Less human effort required and requires less maintenance cost.
5. It has the potential to reduce the countryâ€™s dependence on fossil fuels.
6. Also minimizes the problems associated to the existing waste management techniques followed in Dibrugarh district.


Working of the process:

Step 1: The waste materials are collected from door to door and transported to the Solid Waste Management Plant.
Step 2: The waste materials are segregared in the Plant.
Step 3: The pre-treatment and processing of biodegradable waste is done in the biomethanation plant and the pre-treatment and processing of non-biodegradable waste (excluding glass and metal waste) is done in the pyrolysis plant.
Step 4: Recycling of the glass, metal and ceramic waste is done.
Step 5: New products are made from the recycled materials.
Step 3: The products are supplied to the market again.


Benefits of using the method of biomethanation and pyrolysis for management of waste.

1. Disposal of wastes in landfill areas is one of the major cause of global warming due to production of methane and carbon dioxide which could be reduced significantly by using the method of biomethanation.
2. Using biomethanation and pyrolysis the area required for landfilling can be minimized to a great extent which is one of the major drawbacks of the existing waste management system.
3. It could serve as green source for income to the society.
4. Producing fuels from waste would help in reducing the dependency and concerns related to the use of fossil fuels.
5. Setting up of the biomethanation plant and pyrolysis plant would provide jobs to the local people of the area which in turn improves the economic growth of the country.
6. Ineffective disposal of plastic wastes had forced it to enter in the blood stream of human beings, hence it is very much necessary to mange the plastic waste effectively and pyrolysis is one such method.
